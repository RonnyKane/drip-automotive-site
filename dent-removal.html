PDR & Traditional Dent Repair

Dents Gone — Without Destroying Your Original Paint

Your vehicle left the factory with a perfect finish. Paintless Dent Repair (PDR) is the method that preserves it. Using specialized tools and lighting, our technicians gently massage the metal back to its original position from behind the panel — no filler, no primer, no paint. Just your original finish, restored.

PDR is ideal for hail damage, door dings, shopping cart hits, minor collision dents, and any dent where the paint hasn't been broken or cracked. The result is indistinguishable from undamaged panels — and the repair holds as well as the original metal.

For dents that require filler or repainting (deep creases, dents with cracked paint, or major panel deformation), we offer traditional dent repair with our professional paint process for a seamless finish.

When to Use PDR vs. Traditional Repair

✅ PDR Is Right When:

  • Paint is intact (no cracks or chips)
  • Dent is round or smooth in shape
  • Hail damage (multiple small dents)
  • Door dings and minor parking lot damage
  • Panel is accessible from behind

🔧 Traditional Repair When:

  • Paint is cracked, chipped, or missing
  • Sharp creases requiring metal work
  • Deep dents with stretched metal
  • Panel is too close to frame for access
  • Damage combined with collision repair

Florida Hail Damage Specialists

Central Florida and Lake County get hit with severe storms every year — and hail damage can leave dozens or even hundreds of small dents across the roof, hood, and trunk of your vehicle. Our team specializes in hail repair, using PDR to work systematically across every panel, restoring your vehicle without a single coat of new paint.

If your insurance covers hail damage (most comprehensive policies do), we handle the entire claim process — see our insurance claims page for details.

PDR Advantages

  • Preserves factory paint and finish
  • No risk of mismatched paint
  • Faster repair time
  • Lower cost than traditional repair
