How a Typical Restoration Unfolds

Initial Consultation

We sit down with you, look over the vehicle together, and talk through your goals. What's the end state? Show car? Driver? Restomod? The vision shapes everything that follows.

Assessment & Scope

We do a thorough inspection — documenting rust, body damage, panel fit, paint condition, and structural issues. You receive a detailed scope of work and estimate before anything starts.

Metal Work & Body Prep

Rust is cut out and replaced with new metal. Panels are straightened, filled where needed, and blocked to glass-smooth perfection. For restorations, this phase can take weeks — and it's worth every hour.

Paint Preparation

The entire car is stripped, epoxy-primed, and sealed before any color coats touch the surface. Proper foundation = paint that lasts decades, not years.

Paint Application

Color applied in our professional spray booth, then clear-coated, sanded, compounded, and polished. The level of polish matches your project's goals — from driver-quality to competition-level concours finish.

Final Assembly & Delivery

Trim reinstalled, everything inspected, and photos taken. We walk you through every inch of the work before you drive it away or load it on the trailer.

Restoration Levels

Every Project Has a Right Level

We scope every restoration honestly — no upselling work you don't need, no shortcuts on work that matters.

🧹

Cosmetic Refresh

Driver's-level paint correction, spot repairs, rust touch-up, and a fresh clear coat. Gets a tired classic looking sharp again without a full rebuild commitment.

Good for: Daily drivers, barn finds you want to show, entry-level show prep

🔨

Full Paint & Body

Complete strip-down, rust repair, body work, and a professional quality respray. The sweet spot for most restorations — show-ready without a concours price tag.

Good for: Show cars, restomod builds, vehicles with value worth protecting

🏆

Frame-Off Concours

Every panel removed, every surface addressed. Block-sanded multiple times, museum-grade paint, detailed jambs, trunk, and firewall. The definitive restoration.

Good for: Award-winning show cars, significant collector vehicles, once-in-a-lifetime builds
